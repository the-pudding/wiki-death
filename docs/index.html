The Half-Life of Death

How famous people are remembered (or not) after they die.

By Russell Goldenberg

When a famous person dies, the internet lights up in a display of public mourning. Articles are written, tweets are tweeted, and Wikipedia is updated. It’s a big deal. Despite never having met the deceased, we feel the impact of the loss, and reflect on what that person meant to us.

How big of a deal is it?
